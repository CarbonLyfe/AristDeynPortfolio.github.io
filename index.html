My project is combining the storytelling-focused minimalism of David Mazzuchelli, Alex Toth, and Darwyn Cooke with narrative driven high art movements of the past like Ashcan Realists and German Expressionism. Alex Toth prescribed a "documentarian impressionist style" perfectly coordinating with schools like the Ashcan or even, more abstractly Chekovian impressionism.

"Starting with the famous The Eight [the Ashcan Artists] the comics and modern American Realism had the same roots in illustrated journalism, the chronicling of American life.Pursuing a range of individual styles that absorbed some impressionism and abstraction, they insisted on depicting specific moments of everyday modern existence... a sense of reportage." — Steven Smith. "Cartooning the ‘American Scene’: Comics as Modern Landscape", Panels and Prose.
"Sickles was keen about impressionists—Monet, Sisley, the political cartoonist David Low, who had lush, wonderful lines and spotting rich blacks, way of drawing and staging! His political cartoons were works of art! Sickles was strongly influenced by Sullivant's distortions, realistic and fantastic, at once. Plus the influence of the Simplicissimus German artist-caricaturist-illustrator Thony and Blix, Gulbransen. He took it all in. Some of it infected Caniff's work, echoes of that influence through Sickles...Caniff, on the other hand, was more the cartoonist." — Alex Toth. "A Talk With Alex Toth".Comic Book Artist #11. 1998.

Sequential

Novelistic rhythmic pacing used by artists like Will Eisner and Frank Miller (in Dark Knight Returns specifically), is a heavy influence of mine. The technique capitalizes on a sense of unfolding time, as opposed to splashier styles of comic art where events feel like they're happening simultaneously. This technique heightens camera techniques, as camera movment can only be interpreted through time, making it possible to translate filmmakers like Pier Pasolini, Robert Altman, Dario Argento to paper, similar to artists like Hatsuhiro Otomo and Frank Quitely.

Isolated Sequential

It's little known that the Ashcan school, a fine art movement with their roots in impressionism, realism and cartooning, was the origin point of gag cartoons and an eraly influence on the style of early American comics, both humorous and serious. Alex Toth prescribed artists to use a "documentarian impressionist" style, the exact mission statement of Robert Henri, the father of the Ashcan movement.
