Arist Deyn Portfolio 2026

My project has been to combine the storytelling-focused minimalism of David Mazzuchelli, Alex Toth, and Darwyn Cooke with the Ashcan Realists. It's little known that the Ashcan artists of New York, a group of realist painters with their roots in impressionism, were the genesis of gag cartoons and consequently much of the style of American comics, both humorous and serious. Alex Toth prescribed a "documentarian impressionist style" perfectly coordinating with the Ashcan school.

Sequential

Novelistic rhythmic pacing between tension and release, used by artists like Will Eisner and Frank Miller (in Dark Knight Returns specifically), has influenced me heavily. The technique capitalizes on time unfolding as opposed to the more common contemporary storytelling where collage-like splashy panels seem to occur simultaneously. This interest in unfolding time has also heitened my interest in translating cinematic camera techniques to the page, as influenced by Katsuhiro Otomo and Frank Quitely as well as filmmakers like Pier Pasolini, Robert Altman, Dario Argento and many more.

Isolated Sequential

Combining the style of the Ashcan realists with classic comics is of great interest to me. It's little known that the Ashcan school, a fine art movement with their roots in impressionism, realism and cartooning, was the genesis point of gag cartoons but they were also a deep influence on the style of early American comics, both humorous and serious. Alex Toth prescribed a "documentarian impressionist" style for comics, the exact mission statement of Robert Henri, the father of the Ashcan movement.
