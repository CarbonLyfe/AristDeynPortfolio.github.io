Arist Deyn Portfolio 2026

My name is Arist Deyn, and I'm an artist currently based in Seattle, Washington. My main project as a comic artist is to combine the storytelling-focused minimalism of creators like Roy Crane, Alex Toth, David Mazzuchelli and Darwyn Cooke with narrative driven fine art movements of the past, especially Ashcan Realism and other Post Impressionist movements like German Expressionism. These art movments, along with impressionist and modernist literature are inextricably linked to comics, but that's a dissertation for another time.

Sequential

Novelistic rhythmic pacing used by creators like Will Eisner and Frank Miller (in Dark Knight Returns specifically) has has a profound impact on my storytelling. This technique capitalizes on a sense of unfolding time, as opposed to splashier styles of comic art panels have a sense of simultaneity. This technique heightens cinematic feel, as camera movment is a timed phenomenon, making it possible to translate techniques from filmmakers like Pier Pasolini, Robert Altman, Dario Argento to the page. Katsuhiro Otomo and Frank Quitely have also influenced me on this topic quite a bit.

Isolated Sequential

It's little known that the Ashcan school, a fine art movement with their roots in impressionism, realism and cartooning, was the origin point of gag cartoons and an eraly influence on the style of early American comics, both humorous and serious. Alex Toth prescribed artists to use a "documentarian impressionist" style, the exact mission statement of Robert Henri, the father of the Ashcan movement.
